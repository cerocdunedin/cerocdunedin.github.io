Class Levels

Beginners

Anybody can come along to our beginner classes. Two left feet welcome!  Class starts at 7.15pm every Wednesday. For more details about what to expect, see below.

Intro-mediate

This is the next level up from beginners. We generally recommend coming to at least 10 beginner classes before moving up. To discuss moving to this level, talk to a teacher :) 

Intermediate

This is our most advanced class. When you feel you are ready to move up from intro-mediate, talk to a teacher who can give you feedback and sign you off.

What to expect

Unsure what to expect? Read on...
You'll sign in at the front door as you enter, met by a member of our friendly crew.
For Ceroc classes you don't need to bring a partner, though of course you're more than welcome to!
Classes are taught in a rotational manner, so you'll get to move around the room and dance with different people. This makes it more fun and social, and helps you learn faster.
If you and a partner wish to be non-rotational, this is also fine.
Each class starts with a warm up, then everyone pairs up. Generally males "lead" and females "follow", but this is not a rigid rule.
The teachers will introduce you to the basic feel of the ceroc motion and some key pointers to keep in mind, then you'll be into learning some real moves!
Each beginner class consists of 4 moves. They are taught individually and linked together, with music, throughout the class. Generally one of these moves will be a repeat move from the previous week.
As the class wraps up, the teachers will give any announcements and then encourage you to dance to the rest of a song with your current dance partner - you're into "freestyle"!
After this first song you'll have the opportunity to video a demonstration of the class moves if you wish.
There is half an hour of freestyle (social dancing) after the class, giving you the opportunity to get to know others in the class and practice what you've learnt. Don't be afraid to ask more advanced dancers for a dance, including the teachers and crew! They love dancing with beginners.
Over the weeks you'll build up a repertoire of moves and begin to put them together in different orders as you dance. So everybody tends to dance to songs in different ways. Ceroc is all about leads learning to give direction and suggestions for movement and follows being attentive and responsive - this conversation creates some real magic and makes everyone's dancing unique.
Note that it is not essential to attend every week - though it's great if you do! But don't panic if you can't make it to class all the time.

University Classes

We also run beginner classes through Otago University Clubs and Societies. We generally have 4 blocks of 5 weeks each year (Tuesday evenings). The cost is $33 for 5 classes. Non-students are also welcome! More information and sign up can be found on the OUSA website.
